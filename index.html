Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income


Background:


- The data set is based on 2014 ACS 1-year estimates: https://factfinder.census.gov/faces/nav/jsf/pages/searchresults.xhtml for 52 states in USA. The current data set includes data on rates of income, obesity, poverty, etc. by state.
- Each circle represents a state, hover over the circle will show state's data base on the x and Y labels.
- Click on any x or Y labels will show the relevant data in the scatter chart. There are 9 options to explore.

Analysis:


Healthcare, obesity and smoke
- Texas(TX) has the highest rate for residents lacking healthcare, follow by Georgia(GA).
- Arkansas(AR) is the highest obesitiy rate, closedly follow by West Virginia(WV), Mississippi(MS) and
Lousiana(LA), range from high of 35.9 down to 34.9. They also have a high poverty rate, range from high of 21.5 down to 18.3.
- West Virginia(WV) has the highest smoking rate, followed by Kentucky(KY), range from high of 26.7 down to 26.2, they also have high poverty rate.
- Massachuettes(MA) has the lowest rate for residents lacking healthcare.
- Washington D.C.(DC) has the lowest rate of obesity.

Age, income and poverty
- The median age range is from 30.5 to 44.1, from Utah (UT) to Maine(ME).
- Mississippi(MS), Arkansas(AR), West Virginia(WV) are states that are at the lower income ranges. This correlates to point 2 above, where the obesitity factor also high.
- New Hampshire(NH) has the lowest poverty rate. The highest poverty state is Mississippi(MS), followed by New Mexico(NM).
